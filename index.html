Case study

The Guelph Wish Fund for Children

Website Redesign

The Guelph Wish Fund for Children

Website Redesign

Goals: To analyze the existing website, develop a problem statement, and to iterate designs to solve the current issues with the site.

Methodology: One on one interviews, surveys, heuristic evaluations, usability tests, and A/B tests.

Constraints: Our team (4 members) want to keep the current branding and colour style of the website because small charities often have difficulty rebranding due to the costs of replacing physical items (t-shirts, brochures, etc.)

Timeline: 3 weeks

Role: My role as a UX/UI designer involved creating wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs based on user feedback and testing.

Tool: Figma

Research

User Insights- Charitable Giving Interviews

Alignment with personal values and perceived impact are the most important factors to donating

Users want to have a clear picture of an organization’s overall goals and where their money goes

They wanted a way to give back that fits in with their lifestyle, an easier donation process makes it more likely for them to follow up

Usability Testing- Original GWF Website

Colour scheme made it easy to tell that it was a children’s charity, but it was not clear from the homepage exactly what the charity does

Important information was scattered throughout multiple pages.

Font choice and unaligned images made the site seem less professiona.

Donation process was confusing and not user friendly.

80%

Of survey respondents were not familiar with children’s wish foundations and what they do.

Our User

“If you’ve got the money, why not? It makes you feel good and it’s the right thing to do.”

Pain:

-Doubt and uncertainty about the way some charities use their funds, lack of transparency.

Gains:

-Satisfaction and fulfillment from making a positive impact through donations.

Definition & Ideation

Problem Statement:

How might we redesign the GWF website to clearly communicate their mission of granting wishes to kids under 19 with diseases or disabilities, make it apparent how these wishes improve kids’ mental and physical health, and entice users to donate?

This organization raises funds and plans logistics to provide children with severe medical issues a respite from the constant stress of being sick. We’re better because we add personal touches, such as our birthday card program and Curl for Kids events. We’re believable because we have granted 447 wishes and have a record high of children enrolled in the birthday card program this year.

Low-Fidelity Testing

Users liked…

-The “What We Do” section of the homepage

-That the contact page could be found in the footer



Users wanted us to…

-Reorder the drop down menu categories by importance

-Create a confirmation page before the donation was submitted

-Make the progress tracker on the donation page more clear

Most of the changes users wanted involved making sure all of the components were consistent, clearing up some of the form labels for the donation page, and cleaning up the layout of the community supporter section of the homepage.

Responsive Design

Because our layout consisted of a lot of information boxes, it was easy to adapt to mobile.

The boxes stay horizontally aligned, but are able to be scrolled on a small screen.

Any hover text or animation becomes permanently triggered, ensuring that users can see all of the information.
